  \section{Subspace help.  Here is subspace help}

A {\bf subspace} of a \vectorspace\ is a subset of that vector space
that is closed under addition and scalar multiplication.  Formally,

If $F$ is a \myfield\ and $V$ a \vectorspace\ over $F$, then $W\subseteq
V$ is a subspace of $V$ if:

\begin{itemize}
\item $\boldzero\in W$
\item $\boldu,\boldv\in W\longrightarrow\boldu+\boldv\in W$
\item $\boldu\in W, c\in F\longrightarrow c\boldu\in W$
\end{itemize}

In particular, an \ideal\ is a subspace.

\subsection*{Examples}

Ordinary $\mathbb{R}^3$.
