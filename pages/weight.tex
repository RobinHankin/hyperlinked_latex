  \section{Weight help}

  A {\bf weight} is a generalization of the concept of eigenvalue
  (which applies to a single linear operator) to sets of linear
  operators, such as subalgebras of \glv.
  

\mainbox{Formally, if $A$ is a \subalgebra\ of a \glv, then
  $\lambda\colon A\longrightarrow\mathbb{F}$ is a {\bf weight} for $A$
  if}{V_\lambda=\left\{v\in V\colon a(v)=\lambda(a)(v)\quad\forall
  a\in A\right\}}{is a nonzero subspace of $V$.  Here $V_\lambda$ is
  the {\bf weight space}}
