  \section{Nilpotent Lie algebra help}

  A {\bf Nilpotent lie algebra} is a Lie algebra $L$ for which 
  the \lowercentralseries\ includes the zero Lie algebra.

  \mainbox{Formally, a Lie algebra $L$ is nilpotent if some element of
    the \lowercentralseries\ $L^m=0$}{1+1=2}


  \subsection*{Facts}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Any \subalgebra\ of a nilpotent Lie algebra is nilpotent.
    \item If $L/\centremath(L)$---where $\centremath(L)$ is the
      \centre\ of $L$---is nilpotent then $L$ is nilpotent.
    \item A Lie algebra $L$ is nilpotent iff the \adjoint\ map
      $\operatorname{ad} x$ is nilpotent, for all $x\in L$.
    \item It is possible to find a nilpotent lie algebra $L$ with an
      \ideal\ $I$ where both $L/I$ and $I$ are nilpotent (thus, the
      concept of nilpotence kind of sucks; \semisimple\ is a more
      fruitful property of a Lie algebra).
    \item (Engel's theorem).  A Lie algebra $L$ is nilpotent iff the
      linear map $\adjointmath x\colon L\longrightarrow L$ is a
      nilpotent map for all $x\in L$.
  \end{itemize}
