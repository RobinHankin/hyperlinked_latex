  \section{Lie algebra help Here!}

A {\bf Lie algebra} is a \vectorspace\ over a field $\mathbb{F}$,
equipped with a map $\bracket{\cdot}{\cdot}$, the lie bracket:

\boxeq{L\times L\longrightarrow L,\qquad(x,y)\mapsto\bracket{x}{y}}

where

\begin{itemize}
  \item (bilinearity): $a,b\in\mathbb{F}, a,b\in L\longrightarrow\bracket{ax+by}{z}=a\bracket{x}{z}+b\bracket{y}{z}$
  \item (alternativity): $x\in L\longrightarrow\bracket{x}{x}=0$
  \item (Jacobi identity): $x,y,z\in
    L\longrightarrow\bracket{x}{\bracket{y}{z}}+\bracket{y}{\bracket{z}{x}}+\bracket{z}{\bracket{x}{y}}=0$
\end{itemize}

Bilinearity and alternativity together show that
$\bracket{x}{y}=-\bracket{y}{x}$.

It is usual to use either upper-case Latin letters $L,M,\ldots$ or
lower-case fraktur letters \textfrak{g},\textfrak{h} for Lie algebras.

Other fonts would be textswab and textgoth:

\textfrak{This is textfrak TEXTFRAK sl s:l}

\textswab{This: is: Schwabacher THIS IS SCHWABACHER  sl s:l}

\textgoth{this is textgoth THIS IS TEXTGOTH sl s:l}


\subsection*{Examples}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Let $L=\mathbb{R}^3$ and define $\bracket{x}{y}$ as the vector
    cross product $x\times y$.  The Jacobi identity follows from the
    facts that
    $\boldmath{a}\times\left(\boldmath{a}\times\boldmath{b}\right)=\boldmath{b}\left(\boldmath{a}\cdot\boldmath{c}\right)-\boldmath{c}\left(\boldmath{a}\cdot\boldmath{b}\right)\,\mbox{and}\,\boldmath{a}\cdot\boldmath{b}=\boldmath{b}\cdot\boldmath{a}
    $.




    \end{itemize}
